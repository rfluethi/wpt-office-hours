# Contributing to Make WordPress Training Office Hours

Welcome! There are several ways you can contribute to the Office Hours initiative – no coding skills required.

## New to GitHub?

If you've never used GitHub before, don't worry. You mainly need to know two things:
- **Issues** are like discussion threads where you can propose topics or ask questions
- **Files** can be read directly in the browser – no installation needed

For a gentle introduction, the [GitHub Quickstart guide](https://docs.github.com/en/get-started/quickstart/hello-world) takes about 10 minutes. Or simply reach out in the `#training` channel on [WordPress Slack](https://make.wordpress.org/chat/) – we're happy to help.

---

## Ways to Contribute

### 1. Propose a Meeting Topic

The easiest way to get involved is to suggest a topic for a future meeting.

1. Go to [Issues → New Issue](https://github.com/rfluethi/wpt-office-hours/issues/new?template=topic-proposal.md)
2. Select the **Topic Proposal** template
3. Fill in the details and submit

Your proposal will appear on the [Kanban Board](https://github.com/rfluethi/wpt-office-hours/projects) in the **Ideas** column. The team will review it and move it to **Planned** when it's scheduled.

> **Good first contribution:** Opening a topic proposal issue is a great way to get started. Look for issues tagged [`good first issue`](https://github.com/rfluethi/wpt-office-hours/labels/good%20first%20issue) for other easy entry points.

### 2. Review Meeting Notes

After each meeting, a draft of the notes is published in [`/notes`](notes/) before being posted on the Make WordPress Training blog. You have **approximately 48 hours** to suggest corrections.

**How to comment on a note:**
1. Open the file in [`/notes`](notes/)
2. Click the line you want to comment on (a blue `+` icon appears on the left)
3. Click the `+` and type your comment
4. Alternatively, [open a Document Correction issue](https://github.com/rfluethi/wpt-office-hours/issues/new?template=document-correction.md) and reference the file

Once approved, the notes are published on [make.wordpress.org/training](https://make.wordpress.org/training/).

### 3. Moderate a Meeting

We rotate moderators to share facilitation responsibility and help everyone build confidence.

- No experience required – templates and checklists are provided
- A co-moderator will always support you
- You moderate at most once every 3 months
- You can always decline without explanation

Express your interest in the `#training` Slack channel or by commenting on an upcoming meeting issue.

### 4. Improve this Repository

Found a typo, a broken link, or something that could be clearer? Open an Issue or submit a Pull Request. See the [Pull Request template](.github/PULL_REQUEST_TEMPLATE.md) for what to include.

## Governance

This repository is maintained by [@rfluethi](https://profiles.wordpress.org/rfluethi/) on behalf of the [Make WordPress Training team](https://make.wordpress.org/training/). Decisions about the Office Hours format are made by the team via the weekly Tuesday meeting in the `#training` channel on [WordPress Slack](https://make.wordpress.org/chat/), with input from **Team Representatives** (Team Reps are elected contributors who speak for the team in the Make WordPress project).

Anyone can propose changes via Issues or Pull Requests. Significant format changes are discussed in the Tuesday meeting before being merged.

**Backup maintainer:** If the primary maintainer is unavailable, Team Reps from the Make WordPress Training team will be given repository access as needed. To become a maintainer, express your interest in `#training` on Slack.

## Kanban Board

| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Ideas** | Proposed topics, open for community input |
| **Planned** | Accepted for an upcoming meeting |
| **Done** | Discussed – linked to published meeting notes |

## Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| `topic-proposal` | A suggested topic for a future meeting |
| `good first issue` | A great starting point for new contributors |
| `meta` | Discussion about the Office Hours format itself |
| `help-wanted` | Looking for someone to take on a task |
| `timezone-APAC` | Relevant for an APAC-friendly meeting time |
| `timezone-Americas` | Relevant for an Americas-friendly meeting time |
